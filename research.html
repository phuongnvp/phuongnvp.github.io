Research Interests

Computer-Aided Drug Design


Drug discovery and development is a challenging process that requires huge investments of time and resources, with a high risk of failure along the way. In our lab, we use various computational techniques, including QSAR (Quantitative Structure-Activity Relationship), molecular docking, and molecular dynamics simulations to help speed up this process and increase the chances of success. While these computational methods are powerful, we know they aren't perfect. That's why part of our research also focuses on making these techniques more accurate and reliable. We believe that by improving these tools, we can make the whole drug discovery process more efficient.

Machine Learning in Chemistry


Machine learning has become a powerful tool that's making waves across many scientific fields, including chemistry. In our lab, we're exploring how these this technique can help solve different chemistry problems that are traditionally very time-consuming or difficult to solve and require high level of understanding, i.e., predict whether chemical reactions might happen, retrosynthesis planning, and proposing the mechanisms behind chemical reactions.

Natural Products Extraction


Natural products are fascinating molecules that often have unique structural features and potential biological activities. However, getting these compounds from plants can be tricky as they usually exist in complex mixtures and in very small amounts, making it challenging to isolate them. In our lab, we're working on developing green methods to extract these valuable compounds. We also use computational techniques to improve existing extraction procedures and better understand how these processes work. By combining green extraction methods with computational approaches, we aim to make the whole process more efficient and sustainable.
