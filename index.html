About me

My name is Nguyen Van Phuong. Currently, I am a PhD student at University of Vienna under the supervision of Assoc. Prof. Priv. Doz. Dr. Anna Weinzinger. My research focuses on lipid modulation of membrane proteins and strategies to target these proteins using an integrated approach that combines molecular dynamics simulations with AI/ML workflows. I believe that interesting discoveries will happen when we combine knowledge from various areas. Therefore, I'm really excited in interdisciplinary environments where chemistry and biology meets data science. It's fascinating to see how computer science, chemistry, biology, and other fields can work together to tackle challenging questions in drug discovery and development.

When I'm not working on research, I love getting lost in good novels and movies. Some of my favorites books are Game of Thrones by George R. R. Martin, The Three-Body Problem by Liu Cixin and The Sorrow of War by Bao Ninh. In cinema, I find myself drawn to movies like The Whale (2022), Before Triology (1995-2013) and The Social Network (2010).

Research Interests

Membrane-Protein Modulation

Using molecular dynamics simulations to investigate how lipid environments shape protein structure, dynamics, and function, with the ultimate aim of enabling more selective and effective drug discovery strategies targeting these proteins.

AI-Driven Drug Discovery

Using advanced architectures, such as LLMs, VAEs, and GFlowNets to navigate vast chemical spaces for virtual screening, de novo design and lead optimization, as well as enhance the predictive accuracy of classical QSAR and molecular docking.

Machine Learning in Chemistry

Using machine learning to streamline complex laboratory workflows in chemistry, such as multi-step retrosynthesis planning, proposing reaction mechanisms, suggesting HPLC gradient program and structure elucidation from spectral data.
